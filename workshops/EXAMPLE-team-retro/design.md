# Design — Team retrospective (EXAMPLE)

> Sanitized sample output of the `designer` agent. Shows the format a real `design.md` takes.

**Goal:** Surface what helped/hindered last quarter and agree 1-2 small experiments. · **Medium:** Miro · **Group:** 8 · **Time box:** 60-90 min · **This design:** ~75 min

## Design stance (why this shape)
- **Protect the quiet members** — every step starts with silent individual input, so speaking up isn't required to contribute. → `1-2-4-all`.
- **Defuse the raw incident** — approach the hard stuff through a depersonalized, system-level frame rather than naming people. → `triz`.
- **End on agency, not blame** — debrief lands on small, owned experiments. → `what-so-what-now-what`.
- **Facilitator off the content** — the team's words fill the board, not the lead's. → `tbr-concrete-practice`.

## Agenda (≈75 min)
| # | Time | Block | Structure (practice) | Purpose in this room |
|---|------|-------|----------------------|----------------------|
| 1 | 5′ | Check-in | — (facilitator) | One word: how you arrive. Set "reflection, not blame." |
| 2 | 25′ | What helped / hindered | **1-2-4-All** (`1-2-4-all`) | Silent Miro stickies → pairs → quads → cluster. Quiet voices protected. |
| 3 | 20′ | The hard truth | **Making-Space-with-TRIZ** (`triz`) | "How could we guarantee a bad quarter?" → "what do we already do?" Lets the raw stuff out safely. |
| 4 | 20′ | Experiments | **What/So What/Now What** (`what-so-what-now-what`) | Convert clusters into 1-2 small, owned experiments. |
| 5 | 5′ | Close | Round (facilitator) | Each names the experiment they'll watch. |

**Total: 75 min** — within the 60-90 box.

### Time reconciliation
- At **90′**: widen block 2 to 35′ for deeper clustering.
- At **60′**: trim TRIZ 20→12 and experiments 20→15. Keep blocks 2 and 4 intact (load-bearing).

## Facilitator notes
- Brief the team lead to participate as a peer; the incident is raw, so the lead defending decisions would shut the room down.
- If the team is more analytical than tense, swap TRIZ → **Wicked Questions** (`wicked-questions`).
