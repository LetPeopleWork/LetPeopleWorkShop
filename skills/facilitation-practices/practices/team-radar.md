# Team Radar

- **Source detail:** Esther Derby & Diana Larsen, *Agile Retrospectives* (2006), pp. 71-73. A "Gather Data" activity using a radar/spider chart. Original to the authors.
- **Purpose:** Quantify the team's own perception of how it's doing against a few **named factors**, on a radar chart — and re-run the same chart later so it becomes a **progress measure** over time.
- **When to use:** Gather Data phase, when you want a repeatable, comparable read on specific practices/behaviors the team is trying to improve (e.g. test-first, pairing, clear acceptance criteria).
- **Group config:** Individuals score each spoke → plot (individual marks or group average) → discuss the shape.
- **Timing:** ~15-20 min. Trim with fewer factors.
- **Medium fit:**
  - In-person: a spider chart on a flip; each person marks each spoke. Good.
  - Miro: a radar template with draggable points per person.
  - Video: a shared radar board, or collect scores in chat and plot live.
- **Steps:**
  1. Agree a handful of **factors** to assess — concrete behaviors the team is working on, not vague virtues.
  2. Draw a radar chart, one spoke per factor, scaled (e.g. 0-10).
  3. Each person marks their score on each spoke (or compute the group average).
  4. Discuss the low spokes and the spread; pick what to improve. **Re-run the same radar** in a later retro to see movement.
- **Facilitator notes:** Repeatability is the value — keep the factors stable across retros so the chart tracks progress, not just a one-off mood. The **spread** (disagreement on a factor) is as interesting as a low average: dig into why people score the same thing differently. Don't assess fuzzy virtues ("collaboration") — pick observable behaviors. Cousin of the single-question satisfaction histogram; Team Radar's multi-factor + repeat-measure design is why it earns the slot.
