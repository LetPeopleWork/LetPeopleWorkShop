# 9 Whys

- **Purpose:** Help a group discover the deeper, shared purpose behind its work by repeatedly asking "why is this important?" until the surface answers give way to what actually matters.
- **When to use:** When a team is busy but unclear on *why*, when motivation is fuzzy, or before designing/prioritizing anything — to anchor the work in a purpose people genuinely share.
- **Group config:** Pairs (interview) -> groups of 4 -> whole group harvest.
- **Timing:** ~20 min (pairs ~5 min each way, ~5 min groups, ~5 min whole-group). Trim to ~12 min with one pair round and a fast harvest.
- **Medium fit:**
  - In-person: standing pairs, no notes at first — keep it conversational and probing.
  - Miro: pair frames capturing the "why" ladder, then cluster shared purposes on the board.
  - Video: breakout pairs ask the question live, then groups of 4, then plenary.
- **Steps:**
  1. In pairs: Person A asks Person B, "Why is the work you do important?" Listen to the answer.
  2. Ask "why is *that* important?" again — and again — roughly 5 times, each time going deeper.
  3. Switch roles and repeat so both partners are interviewed.
  4. In groups of 4, share the fundamental purposes uncovered and notice what's common.
  5. Whole group: harvest the deeper purposes that resonate across everyone.
- **Facilitator notes:** The power is in the *repetition* — the first two "whys" produce slogans; the real purpose only surfaces around the fourth or fifth. Do NOT let pairs stop after one or two answers because it feels redundant; the redundancy is the dig. Coach interviewers to keep asking even when it gets uncomfortable.
