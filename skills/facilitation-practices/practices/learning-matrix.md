# Learning Matrix

- **Source detail:** Esther Derby & Diana Larsen, *Agile Retrospectives* (2006), pp. 99-101. A "Generate Insights" activity; the named ancestor of the popular "4 Ls" / four-quadrant retro family. Original to the authors.
- **Purpose:** Capture insight fast across four framings at once — what to keep, what to change, new ideas, and people to appreciate — so analysis doesn't collapse into a pure problem-list.
- **When to use:** Generate Insights phase of a retro, or any quick structured reflection where you want balanced (not only negative) insight in a short slot.
- **Group config:** Silent individual write → post to quadrants → cluster → dot-vote what to carry forward.
- **Timing:** ~20-25 min. Trim by shrinking the write and the vote.
- **Medium fit:**
  - In-person: a flip chart in four quadrants; sticky notes; dots to vote. Excellent.
  - Miro: a four-quadrant frame; stickies + voting.
  - Video: shared four-quadrant board, write then post, poll to vote.
- **Steps:**
  1. Draw four quadrants: **:) keep / what worked** · **:( change / what didn't** · **💡 new ideas** · **💐 appreciate (people to thank)**.
  2. Individuals **silently** write stickies and post to quadrants.
  3. Cluster; briefly walk each quadrant.
  4. **Dot-vote** the items to carry into Decide What to Do.
- **Facilitator notes:** The four named quadrants do the analytic work fast, and the "new ideas" and "appreciate" cells stop it being purely problem-focused (which protects morale and surfaces strengths). Keep the write silent-first so the loudest voice doesn't seed the quadrants. Sits in the `five-phase-retrospective` Generate Insights phase; lighter than a full root-cause analysis when time is short.
