# ESVP (Explorer / Shopper / Vacationer / Prisoner)

- **Source detail:** Esther Derby & Diana Larsen, *Agile Retrospectives* (2006), pp. 45-47. A "Set the Stage" opener that reads the room's attitude toward the session. Original to the authors.
- **Purpose:** Surface — *anonymously* — how engaged people actually are as they arrive, so hidden disengagement is named and addressed before the real work, instead of silently sabotaging it.
- **When to use:** Opening a retro or workshop where attendance may be reluctant or mandated, and you need an honest read on buy-in and psychological safety.
- **Group config:** Anonymous individual write → facilitator tallies a histogram → whole-group debrief.
- **Timing:** ~10-15 min. Trim by shortening the debrief.
- **Medium fit:**
  - In-person: explain the four, everyone writes a letter on a folded slip, collect in a hat, tally on the board. Anonymity is easy.
  - Miro: an anonymous vote (hidden until revealed) into four buckets.
  - Video: an anonymous poll tool — essential; a show of hands would break anonymity.
- **Steps:**
  1. Explain the four attitudes: **Explorer** (eager to discover and learn) · **Shopper** (happy to leave with one useful thing) · **Vacationer** (glad to be away from work, not really engaged) · **Prisoner** (here only because forced).
  2. Each person **anonymously** writes their letter on a slip; collect.
  3. Tally a histogram in front of the group — never identify anyone.
  4. Debrief the pattern: "what does this tell us? how is this like how we show up day-to-day?" If engagement is low, renegotiate the session.
- **Facilitator notes:** Anonymity is non-negotiable — the whole point is an honest read of buy-in, and a Prisoner will never self-identify in the open. Destroy the slips visibly afterward to reinforce safety. If the room is mostly Vacationers and Prisoners, *name it and address it* rather than ploughing on — that conversation is more valuable than the agenda. Pairs with the `four-player-model` lens for reading the dynamics that follow.
