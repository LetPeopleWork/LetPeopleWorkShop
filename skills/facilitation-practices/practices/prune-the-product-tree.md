# Prune the Product Tree

- **Source detail:** Luke Hohmann, *Innovation Games* (registered trademark — technique attributed; no branded kits). A roadmap-shaping game using a tree metaphor.
- **Purpose:** Shape a balanced roadmap by drawing the product as a tree and letting people add desired features as leaves on the limb they grow from — making the *shape* of growth (coherent vs. scattered, lopsided vs. balanced) visible.
- **When to use:** Roadmap/portfolio conversations with stakeholders; when a flat backlog hides whether proposed work hangs together or sprawls.
- **Group config:** Whole group adds leaves to a shared tree -> read the shape -> prune/prioritize together.
- **Timing:** ~30-60 min (draw + brief 10, add leaves 15-20, read shape + decide 15-20). Trim with a pre-drawn tree.
- **Medium fit:**
  - In-person: a big tree on a wall; leaf-shaped cards pinned to limbs. Excellent and tactile.
  - Miro: a tree image with leaf stickies dragged onto branches.
  - Video: works only via a shared board — a plain call loses the spatial metaphor that does the work; prefer in-person/Miro.
- **Steps:**
  1. Draw a big tree: trunk = the product/system core, main limbs = major functional areas, the outer canopy edge = the current release/state.
  2. Give people **leaf cards**; they write desired future features/capabilities and pin each to the limb it grows from.
  3. Read the shape: which branches are growing (lots of leaves), which are bare, whether growth is balanced or lopsided.
  4. Use the shape to shape the roadmap — prune, and prioritize which branches to grow.
- **Facilitator notes:** The tree metaphor surfaces *where* growth clusters and whether it's coherent (organic growth along a branch) or scattered across the canopy — a pattern a flat backlog hides. The bare and the overgrown branches are the conversation. Roadmap-oriented and somewhat niche, but distinct from generic prioritization (`25-10-crowd-sourcing`, `buy-a-feature`): it's about *structural balance*, not just ranking.
