# Wise Crowds

- **Purpose:** Tap the collective intelligence of a small group to help one person with a real challenge, fast — the group becomes an instant consulting panel for each member in turn.
- **When to use:** Members have concrete challenges (a stuck decision, a thorny problem) and would benefit from focused peer consultation; great for making expertise instantly accessible across a team.
- **Group config:** Sets of 4-5. One "client" per round; the rest are "consultants." Rotate so everyone gets a turn.
- **Timing:** ~15 min per client (2 min present, 3 min clarify, ~8 min consult, 2 min reflect). With a full set of 4 rotating, ~50 min total. Trim by shortening rounds or running fewer clients.
- **Medium fit:**
  - In-person: client physically turns their chair away from the group during consultation. Excellent — the turn makes the silence real.
  - Miro: client mutes/steps back; consultants think aloud on a shared frame. Good.
  - Video: client turns off camera and stays muted while consultants discuss on screen. Works well — the camera-off cleanly enforces the listen-only role.
- **Steps:**
  1. The client states their challenge and the help they want (2 min).
  2. Consultants ask clarifying questions; client answers (3 min).
  3. The client turns away (or off camera) and stays silent, listening, while consultants discuss the challenge among themselves — asking questions, offering ideas, sharing observations — as if the client weren't there (~8 min).
  4. The client turns back and shares what was most valuable (2 min).
  5. Rotate to the next client.
- **Facilitator notes:** The client-listens-silently step is the engine. When the client can't respond or defend, consultants talk more freely and the client hears the conversation without needing to react — they catch nuances they'd have argued past. Enforce the turn-away strictly; the instant the client jumps back in to clarify, it collapses into ordinary advice-giving. Insist each client asks for a SPECIFIC kind of help, or consultations wander.
