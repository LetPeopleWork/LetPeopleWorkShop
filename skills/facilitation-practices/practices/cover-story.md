# Cover Story

- **Source detail:** *Gamestorming* (Gray, Brown, Macanufo). A visioning game using the device of a future magazine cover.
- **Purpose:** Surface a group's boldest shared aspiration by imagining its success is already famous — the cover story of a respected magazine — and writing the story in past tense.
- **When to use:** Visioning and alignment; when you want energy and ambition (not a step-by-step plan), and "what's our goal?" produces timid answers.
- **Group config:** Small groups (4-6) each build one cover -> present -> harvest shared themes.
- **Timing:** ~45-90 min (~5 min solo prime, 30-45 group build, 10-15 present + harvest). Trim to ~30 with one small group and a tight template.
- **Medium fit:**
  - In-person: a large magazine-cover template per group; markers + stickies for headlines, quotes, images. Excellent and energising.
  - Miro: a cover-template frame per breakout; fill the zones.
  - Video: works only via a shared board; a plain call loses the visual device — prefer in-person/Miro.
- **Steps:**
  1. Set the future scene: "It's [future date]. Our work has been a huge success — it's the cover story of [a magazine your audience respects]."
  2. Small groups fill a cover template in **past tense**: the big headline, the cover image, sidebar stories, pull-quotes from customers/critics, key insights.
  3. Groups present their covers.
  4. Harvest the recurring themes across covers — the shared aspiration is the real output.
- **Facilitator notes:** The magazine-cover device gives permission to be boldly aspirational without feeling naive — people will write a "headline" they'd never dare state as a "goal." Past tense plus a *real* publication name make it concrete. Overlaps `remember-the-future` and `design-storyboards` in intent; reach for Cover Story when you specifically want collective vision and energy. The harvest (shared themes) matters more than any single cover — don't skip it.
