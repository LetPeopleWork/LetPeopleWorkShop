# Speed Boat (Sailboat)

- **Source detail:** Luke Hohmann, *Innovation Games* (registered trademark — technique attributed; no branded materials). Circulates generically as the **"Sailboat" retrospective**; *Gamestorming* catalogs it as "Speedboat."
- **Purpose:** Make impediments visible and discussable through a boat-and-anchors metaphor — what's slowing the team/product down, with anchor depth signalling severity.
- **When to use:** Retrospectives and discovery; when "what's wrong?" feels too blunt or personal and you want honest impediments surfaced fast and depersonalized.
- **Group config:** Whole group adds anchors to a shared drawing -> cluster -> discuss the heaviest -> agree a first cut.
- **Timing:** ~30-45 min (draw + brief 5, add anchors 10, cluster + discuss 15-20, pick + commit 5). Trim to ~20 with anchors-only.
- **Medium fit:**
  - In-person: a big boat drawn on a wall; anchor cards placed at varying depths. Excellent — visual and tactile.
  - Miro: a boat template; drag anchor stickies, depth = severity; add wind/rocks for the fuller variant.
  - Video: works only via a shared board (Miro/whiteboard); a plain call loses the visual metaphor that does the work — prefer in-person/Miro.
- **Steps:**
  1. Draw a boat on water; the boat = the team/product/goal.
  2. **Anchors = what's holding us back.** Each person writes impediments on anchor cards and places them — *deeper anchor = bigger drag*.
  3. (Optional Sailboat variant) add **wind/sails** for what's propelling you and **rocks** ahead for upcoming risks.
  4. Cluster, discuss the heaviest anchors, pick one or two to cut, and agree a first step.
- **Facilitator notes:** The anchor metaphor **depersonalizes** complaints — people critique "what's slowing the boat," not a colleague, which surfaces honest impediments faster than a blank retro. The bit people skip is anchor *depth*; insist on it so you can prioritize by severity. Adding wind and rocks gives a fuller retro but can overload a short session — anchors alone are the high-value core. Pick one canonical form (Speed Boat vs full Sailboat) and stick to it.
