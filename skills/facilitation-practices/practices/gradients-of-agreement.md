# Gradients of Agreement

- **Source detail:** Sam Kaner et al., *Facilitator's Guide to Participatory Decision-Making* (Community at Work). The 8-point scale is the canonical version; the 4-point "love it / like it / live with it / loathe it" and "Fist of Five" are folk descendants — cite Kaner.
- **Purpose:** Replace a binary yes/no vote with a graded scale so the *strength and distribution* of support for a specific proposal becomes visible — surfacing lukewarm agreement and quiet reservations before they sink the decision later.
- **When to use:** Closing in on a decision, when a show of hands would hide how people really feel; any time you suspect polite nods are masking "I'll live with it" or hidden blocks. The antidote to a counterfeit yes.
- **Group config:** Whole group signals simultaneously; facilitator reads the spread and talks to the outliers.
- **Timing:** ~10-20 min (state proposal + threshold, simultaneous signal, discuss outliers, re-test). Trim by skipping the re-test; expand with a second refine-and-re-poll round.
- **Medium fit:**
  - In-person: cards or a hand-signal scale (thumb height / fingers); a wall continuum people place a dot on. Excellent — the simultaneous reveal is easy.
  - Miro: a labelled gradient line; everyone drags a token at once on a count of three.
  - Video: a poll (8 or 4 options) revealed together, or everyone shows fingers on camera at once. Works well remotely.
- **Steps:**
  1. Write the proposal as ONE clear, specific statement everyone can see — vague proposals produce vague agreement.
  2. Announce the **decision rule BEFORE polling** (e.g. "we proceed if no one is below 'support with reservations' and there are no blocks").
  3. On a count, everyone signals at once on the scale: endorse / endorse with minor reservation / support with reservations / agree with major reservations / abstain / stand aside / formal disagreement (no block) / block.
  4. Read the spread. Talk to the people lowest on the scale — ask "what would move you up?", not "why do you disagree?".
  5. Refine the proposal and re-poll, or proceed once the threshold is met.
- **Facilitator notes:** The **simultaneous reveal** is the anti-conformity engine — poll one at a time and people anchor on the boss. The gold is the *middle* of the scale: "support with reservations" is a real, buildable yes; a hidden "live with it" you mistook for enthusiasm is exactly how agreements die in practice. Set the threshold *before* the vote or you'll move the goalposts to get the answer you want. Don't average the signals — one block outweighs ten endorsements; treat it as data to explore, not a veto to resent.
