# Social Network Webbing

- **Purpose:** Map the real web of relationships and connections needed to advance a goal, then plan how to activate it — surfacing who actually has influence, not who sits where on the org chart.
- **When to use:** When progress depends on people and connections beyond the room, and the group keeps engaging the "official" stakeholders while the people who could really unblock things stay untouched.
- **Group config:** Individuals map their connections -> small groups merge into a shared web -> whole group spots gaps and key nodes -> plan activation steps.
- **Timing:** ~60 min (individual mapping 15, merge into a shared web 20, read the web for gaps/nodes 10, plan activation 15). Trim to ~25 min for a single small group mapping one goal.
- **Medium fit:**
  - In-person: a big paper/wall web — names as nodes, lines as relationships, drawn collaboratively. The mess of lines is the point.
  - Miro: nodes as cards, connectors as lines; easy to rearrange and cluster, and the web persists for later activation tracking.
  - Video: shared whiteboard to co-draw the web; works well since everyone can add nodes simultaneously.
- **Steps:**
  1. Name the goal that requires support from a network of people.
  2. Each person maps the people they know who are connected to this goal — and crucially the connections BETWEEN those people, not just to themselves.
  3. Merge individual maps into one shared web. Add relationships people discover they have in common.
  4. Read the web: who are the well-connected hubs? Where are the gaps and the people nobody in the room can reach? Who is influential but currently unengaged?
  5. Plan concrete steps to activate the web — who reaches whom, by when, to do what.
- **Facilitator notes:** Insist that people map REAL ties (who actually talks to / trusts / influences whom), not formal roles or reporting lines — the org chart is exactly the map this structure is meant to replace. The richest insight is usually a peripheral, low-status, well-connected person nobody had on the stakeholder list. Don't stop at the pretty picture: step 5 (activation plan with names and dates) is what turns the map into movement.
