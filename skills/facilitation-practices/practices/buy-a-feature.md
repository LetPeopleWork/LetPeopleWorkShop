# Buy a Feature

- **Source detail:** Luke Hohmann, *Innovation Games* (registered trademark — technique attributed; no branded kits). A collaborative-prioritization game.
- **Purpose:** Reveal true priorities by making people **spend a limited budget** on features that are priced by cost/effort — scarcity and the need to pool money force real trade-offs and negotiation that a show of hands never surfaces.
- **When to use:** Prioritizing a backlog, roadmap, or option set with stakeholders, when dot-voting feels too cheap and you want to see what people will actually *trade* for.
- **Group config:** Individuals with budgets -> negotiate and pool to afford pricier items -> debrief as whole group.
- **Timing:** ~30-60 min (set-up + brief 10, buying/negotiating 20-30, debrief 10-15). Trim with fewer features and smaller groups.
- **Medium fit:**
  - In-person: feature cards on a table with prices; play money/tokens; people physically place money. Excellent — the haggling is the value.
  - Miro: priced feature cards + token counters dragged onto them; talk in a call.
  - Video: shared board with prices, breakout to negotiate, plenary tally.
- **Steps:**
  1. Prepare candidate features, each **priced by cost/effort** — price the best ones high enough that no individual can afford them alone.
  2. Give each participant a **fixed budget** (less than the total cost of everything).
  3. People spend — alone, or **pooling money** to afford pricier items, which forces negotiation and coalitions.
  4. Debrief what got funded and, more importantly, the trade-offs and arguments — that's where the real priorities show.
- **Facilitator notes:** Scarcity + the need to **pool** is the engine — price the most valuable features so they can only be bought collectively, forcing people to advocate and trade. That negotiation surfaces genuine willingness-to-pay that `25-10-crowd-sourcing` dot-voting can't. Don't make budgets so generous everyone buys their pet feature — then it's just a wishlist with money. Listen to the *arguments*, not just the totals.
