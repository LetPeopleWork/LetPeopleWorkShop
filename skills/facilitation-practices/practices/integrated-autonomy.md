# Integrated~Autonomy

- **Purpose:** Move a group past a stuck either/or fight (centralize vs decentralize, standard vs custom, global vs local) by finding BOTH/AND solutions that are simultaneously more integrated AND more autonomous.
- **When to use:** A polarized "it has to be one or the other" debate where each camp is defending half a truth and the trade-off feels forced. When endless centralize-vs-decentralize swings have become a pendulum nobody wins.
- **Group config:** Whole group frames the question -> small groups generate on each pole independently -> whole group integrates into both/and ideas.
- **Timing:** ~60 min (frame the tension 10, brainstorm benefits of each pole 15, generate both/and ideas 20, harvest + select 15). Trim to ~40 min by shortening the pole brainstorm and harvesting fewer ideas.
- **Medium fit:**
  - In-person: two walls/flipcharts for the two poles, then a third space for both/and ideas. The physical separation of the poles makes the reframe visible.
  - Miro: three zones — left pole benefits, right pole benefits, center both/and column. Drag winning ideas to center.
  - Video: breakouts, one per pole, then plenary to merge. Keep a shared doc with the three zones.
- **Steps:**
  1. Name the apparent either/or choice as a tension between two genuine goods (e.g. "standardized" AND "customized"). Make clear neither pole is the enemy.
  2. Small groups list the real benefits and necessities of EACH pole on its own — what would we lose if we gave it up?
  3. Ask the reframe question: "How can we get MORE of both at the same time? What design gives us integration AND autonomy?" Generate concrete both/and ideas.
  4. Whole group harvests the both/and ideas, clusters them, and selects the most promising to pursue.
- **Facilitator notes:** The whole structure lives or dies on the reframe in step 3 — from "which one?" to "how do we get both?". Do NOT let the group collapse back into trade-off/compromise language ("let's do 60% central, 40% local"); that is splitting the difference, not integration. Force the genuinely-both answer. Legitimizing each pole in step 2 first is what disarms the two camps so they'll build together.
