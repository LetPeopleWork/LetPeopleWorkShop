# Critical Uncertainties

- **Purpose:** Develop strategies that hold up across multiple plausible futures by building a 2x2 scenario matrix from the two factors that are BOTH most impactful AND most uncertain, then stress-testing strategy in each quadrant.
- **When to use:** Strategy under deep uncertainty — when you can't predict which way key forces will break and want a plan that survives more than one future, not a bet on the one you hope for.
- **Group config:** Whole group lists factors -> selects two critical uncertainties -> small groups each take one of four scenarios -> whole group harvests robust + hedging strategies.
- **Timing:** ~75 min (list factors 10, pick the two axes 15, build the four scenarios in groups 25, develop strategies 15, harvest 10). Trim to ~50 min by pre-listing candidate factors and assigning quadrants faster.
- **Medium fit:**
  - In-person: stickies to list factors, then draw the 2x2 on a large wall; a flipchart per quadrant for the breakout groups.
  - Miro: a factor-dump frame, then a 2x2 frame with four quadrant areas, one breakout group per quadrant.
  - Video: plenary to choose axes, four breakouts (one per scenario), shared doc with a quadrant page each.
- **Steps:**
  1. List the forces/factors that could shape the outcome. Plot each on two scales: how IMPACTFUL and how UNCERTAIN.
  2. Choose the TWO factors that score highest on both impact and uncertainty. These become the two axes of a 2x2.
  3. The two axes create four distinct future scenarios. Small groups each take one quadrant and flesh it out into a vivid, named story.
  4. For each scenario, ask: "What strategy would succeed HERE?" Develop strategies per quadrant.
  5. Whole group harvests: which strategies are robust (work across multiple futures) vs. which are hedges for a single future. Decide what to commit to now.
- **Facilitator notes:** The single hardest discipline is step 2 — people grab the most impactful factor even when it's fairly predictable. A factor you can forecast does NOT belong on an axis; only impact-AND-uncertainty earns a spot. The payoff is in step 5: the goal isn't to predict the right future, it's to find the moves that pay off no matter which future arrives.
