# The Diamond of Participatory Decision-Making

- **Source detail:** Sam Kaner et al., *Facilitator's Guide to Participatory Decision-Making* (Community at Work). The single most-cited map of how groups actually reach sound, supported decisions.
- **Purpose:** Give a decision session a **shape** — open up (diverge), endure the hard middle (the Groan Zone), then narrow down (converge) to a decision — so you can place each structure where it belongs in the group's journey.
- **When to use:** As the macro-arc for any **decision- or problem-solving** session involving real disagreement. Use it to reason about *where* an activity sits, not as a timed block itself.
- **Medium fit:** all — it's a sequencing lens, not an activity.

## The three zones (+ closure)
1. **Divergent Zone** — open up: surface diverse perspectives, options, data, and dissent. Resist deciding. -> map: `1-2-4-all`, `triz`, `impromptu-networking`, `nine-whys`, brainstorming structures.
2. **Groan Zone** — the hard middle: competing frames grind against each other; confusion, repetition, frustration. This is **not failure** — it's where genuine integration happens. -> map: build shared understanding (`conversation-cafe`, `nine-whys`), strengthen relationships, normalize the discomfort.
3. **Convergent Zone** — narrow down: organize, prioritize, refine, select. -> map: `min-specs`, `25-10-crowd-sourcing`, `agreement-certainty-matrix`, `gradients-of-agreement`.
- **Closure** — name the **decision rule** and make the call (often `gradients-of-agreement`).

## How to use it
Place each agenda block in a zone. Never sequence a convergent structure before the group has actually diverged and worked through the Groan Zone, or you'll "converge" on the loudest opinion. Tell the group the Groan Zone is coming so no one panics when it arrives.

## Facilitator notes
The whole value is **naming the Groan Zone** so you don't panic in it. Inexperienced facilitators feel the confusion at the middle and either bail back to more divergence or force a premature vote — both abort the integration that makes a decision stick. Bless the discomfort out loud. Cousin of Gamestorming's open/explore/close arc, but better-sourced and decision-specific — prefer this. Pairs naturally with `gradients-of-agreement` at closure and the `four-player-model` lens for reading the room mid-Groan-Zone.
