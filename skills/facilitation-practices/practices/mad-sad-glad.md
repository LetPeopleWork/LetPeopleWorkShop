# Mad Sad Glad

- **Source detail:** Esther Derby & Diana Larsen, *Agile Retrospectives* (2006), pp. 61-62. A "Gather Data" activity that surfaces emotion as data. (Many variants: Glad-Sad-Mad, the "4 Ls," plus/minus — same idea.)
- **Purpose:** Bring feelings into the room *as data* — the moments over a period that made people mad, sad, or glad — because "feelings are at least half the story" and direct questions about feelings tend to shut people down.
- **When to use:** The Gather Data phase of a retrospective, or any review where the emotional texture of the period matters and a purely factual recap would miss what people actually carry.
- **Group config:** Silent individual write → post to three areas → cluster → read together.
- **Timing:** ~15-25 min. Trim with a shorter write and fewer clusters.
- **Medium fit:**
  - In-person: three posters (Mad / Sad / Glad); color-coded cards. Excellent.
  - Miro: three labelled columns/frames; stickies.
  - Video: shared board with three areas; breakout-free, write then post.
- **Steps:**
  1. Put up three areas: **Mad**, **Sad**, **Glad**.
  2. Individuals **silently** write moments/events from the period that made them feel each (one per card).
  3. Post to the matching area; cluster similar cards.
  4. Read the clusters; carry the ones with the most energy into Generate Insights.
- **Facilitator notes:** The move is surfacing emotion as **data, not venting** — naming the *event* that caused a feeling is the safe indirection (people resist "how do you feel?"). Keep it about events, not people. The clusters (shared feelings) matter more than any single card — they point at the patterns worth analyzing. Sits in the `five-phase-retrospective` Gather Data phase; `speed-boat` covers impediments, this covers feelings.
