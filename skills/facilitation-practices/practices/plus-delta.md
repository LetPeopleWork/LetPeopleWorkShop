# Plus / Delta

- **Source detail:** Esther Derby & Diana Larsen, *Agile Retrospectives* (2006), pp. 114-116. A "Close" activity — a retro-on-the-retro.
- **Purpose:** Improve the *session/meeting itself* in under fifteen minutes — what to keep (**+**) and what to change (**Δ**) next time — in a fast, non-blaming two-column form.
- **When to use:** To close a retro, workshop, or recurring meeting; whenever you want to get better at *how you meet*, not just what you decided.
- **Group config:** Whole group contributes to two shared columns → commit to one or two changes.
- **Timing:** ~10-20 min. Already minimal; trim by capping items per column.
- **Medium fit:**
  - In-person: a "T" chart on a flip — **+** left, **Δ** right; call out or post stickies. Fast.
  - Miro: a two-column frame.
  - Video: a shared two-column doc/board, or round-robin into chat.
- **Steps:**
  1. Draw a **T**: left column **+** (keep — what worked about *this session*), right column **Δ** (change for next time).
  2. People call out or post items to each column — no debating, just capture.
  3. Pick **one or two deltas** to actually apply next session, and note them.
- **Facilitator notes:** This improves the **meeting**, not the work — that's what separates it from the "Now what?" of `what-so-what-now-what` (which is about the team's actual work). The two-column T deliberately caps volume and keeps it non-blaming. Fast and high-reuse: a clean close for almost any session. For a value rating of the session instead of keep/change items, use `roti`.
