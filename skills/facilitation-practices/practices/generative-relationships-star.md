# Generative Relationships STAR

- **Purpose:** Diagnose why a working relationship or collaboration is — or isn't — producing surprising value, by assessing it on four factors instead of pointing fingers at people.
- **When to use:** A team, partnership, or cross-group collaboration feels stuck, flat, or quietly dysfunctional and you want to understand the pattern without it turning into a blame session.
- **Group config:** Small groups of 4-7 who share the relationship being assessed; rate, discuss, then harvest to All.
- **Timing:** ~30 min (5 min explain STAR, 10 min rate + discuss, 10 min find improvements, harvest). Expand to ~40 min with a richer harvest; trim to ~25 min by rating only the weakest factor.
- **Medium fit:**
  - In-person: a drawn STAR diagram on flip chart, dots placed on each axis. Excellent.
  - Miro: a STAR template with sliders/dots per factor and sticky notes for evidence. Strong — the saved board doubles as a follow-up baseline.
  - Video: shared STAR slide, rate via poll or annotation, discuss in breakouts. Works well.
- **Steps:**
  1. Introduce the four STAR factors of a generative relationship: **S**eparateness (enough diversity of perspective?), **T**uning (do we listen and reflect together?), **A**ction (do we try things together?), **R**eason to work together (is the shared purpose compelling?).
  2. Each group rates their relationship on each of the four factors (e.g. low-to-high on each arm of the star).
  3. Discuss: which factors are strong, which weak, and what evidence supports each rating?
  4. For the weakest factors, generate concrete ways to strengthen them.
  5. Harvest patterns and chosen improvements to the whole group.
- **Facilitator notes:** The four factors are what keep it blameless — people critique the *relationship's properties*, not each other's character, which lets stuck partners speak honestly. The usual culprit is too little Separateness (everyone thinks alike) or no real Reason; push groups past "we get along fine" to rate each arm distinctly. Don't let them rate generically — anchor every score to a specific recent interaction or it stays vague.
