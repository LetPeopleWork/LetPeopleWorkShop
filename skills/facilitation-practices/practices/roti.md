# Return on Time Invested (ROTI)

- **Source detail:** Esther Derby & Diana Larsen, *Agile Retrospectives* (2006), pp. 124-126 (citing the authors' earlier work). A meta-evaluation of the *session itself*.
- **Purpose:** Find out whether a meeting or session was **worth the time** people spent on it — a quick, public 0-4 value rating plus the reasons behind it — so you can improve future sessions and stay accountable for people's time.
- **When to use:** To close almost any meeting, workshop, or retro; especially valuable for recurring sessions you want to earn their slot.
- **Group config:** Whole group rates simultaneously; facilitator tallies and probes the extremes.
- **Timing:** ~5-10 min. Hard to trim further; it's already a fast close.
- **Medium fit:**
  - In-person: everyone shows fingers (0-4) at once, or dots on a 0-4 scale on the wall. Fast.
  - Miro: a 0-4 column chart people drop a token into.
  - Video: fingers on camera together, or a 0-4 poll.
- **Steps:**
  1. Ask each person to rate the session's value to them, **0-4**: 0 = total waste / lost time · 1 = barely worth it · 2 = break-even · 3 = good return · 4 = high return (better than the alternative use of the time).
  2. Reveal **simultaneously**; tally a quick histogram.
  3. Ask **high**-raters what benefit they got, and **low**-raters what they'd have needed to make it worthwhile.
  4. Note the asks; act on them next time.
- **Facilitator notes:** Almost nothing else measures whether a session earned its time — asking out loud makes *you* accountable. The gold is the follow-up question, not the number: a "1" with "we never actually decided anything" tells you exactly what to fix. Reveal simultaneously so people don't anchor on the boss or on politeness. Generalizes far beyond retros — use it to prune or fix any recurring meeting.
