# Open Space Technology

- **Purpose:** Let participants self-organize a marketplace of breakout sessions around a central theme, so the people who care about each topic dive into it together — generating ownership and momentum no pre-set agenda could.
- **When to use:** A big, complex, important challenge with no obvious answer, where engagement and diverse contribution matter more than control; works best when leadership can genuinely live with what emerges.
- **Group config:** Whole group convenes -> self-selects into many concurrent breakout sessions -> reconvenes. Sessions run in parallel across the available time/space grid.
- **Timing:** Half-day (~90 min minimum to feel real) up to multiple days (480+ min). The agenda-building opening and the closing harvest are fixed; the number and length of session rounds expand to fill the time. Don't compress below ~90 min or there's no room for the self-organization to pay off.
- **Medium fit:**
  - In-person: a central circle for agenda-building, a posted time/space grid, and enough breakout rooms/corners. Excellent — the native habitat.
  - Video: a shared agenda board plus many breakout rooms people join freely; reconvene in plenary. Works for the self-organizing logic, but the Law of Two Feet is clumsier (people hesitate to leave a call) and energy is harder to sustain across long durations.
  - Miro: as the agenda marketplace + session-notes board alongside live calls, yes — but the structure itself is live convening, not a board activity, so it's a companion, not the medium.
- **Steps:**
  1. State the theme and the invitation. Gather everyone in a circle around an empty time/space grid (slots x spaces).
  2. Anyone who wants to host a session steps in, names their topic, writes it on a card, and posts it to a slot on the grid. Repeat until the marketplace is full.
  3. Announce the four principles ("Whoever comes are the right people," etc.) and the one Law of Two Feet: if you're neither learning nor contributing, move to where you can.
  4. Run the breakout sessions in parallel; each session captures notes/actions.
  5. Reconvene for harvest: sessions post their notes; the group reads, reflects, and converges on next steps (often closing in the circle).
- **Facilitator notes:** The self-organized agenda IS the structure — your job is to hold the space and then get out of the way; the urge to curate or assign topics kills it. Teach the Law of Two Feet explicitly and bless leaving a session out loud, or people sit politely in sessions that aren't serving them. It needs real time and space; squeezing OST into an hour just produces a frustrated crowd. Capture session notes as you go, or the harvest evaporates.
