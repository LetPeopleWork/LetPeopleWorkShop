# Empathy Map

- **Source detail:** Created by **XPLANE** (Dave Gray's firm), popularized by Osterwalder's *Business Model Generation*; catalogued in *Gamestorming*. Attribute to XPLANE, not Gamestorming.
- **Purpose:** Build a shared, concrete picture of a specific stakeholder's experience — what they see, hear, say, do, think, and feel — so the team designs from the stakeholder's reality instead of its own assumptions.
- **When to use:** Before designing anything for a user/customer/stakeholder; to align a team on who they're serving; to surface unmet needs hiding in the gap between what someone says and what they feel.
- **Group config:** Solo or small groups fill one map per stakeholder -> compare.
- **Timing:** ~15-30 min for a rough map. Trim by pre-naming the stakeholder; expand by mapping several personas in parallel.
- **Medium fit:**
  - In-person: a big head drawn on a flip/wall, zones labelled, stickies in each. Excellent.
  - Miro: the standard empathy-map template; stickies per zone.
  - Video: shared template, breakouts per stakeholder, plenary compare.
- **Steps:**
  1. Name the stakeholder/persona and the situation you're mapping.
  2. Draw a head; divide into zones — **Thinking & Feeling, Seeing, Hearing, Saying & Doing**, plus **Pains** and **Gains**.
  3. Project into their perspective; fill each zone with concrete sensory detail (what they literally see/hear/say/do; what they think and feel).
  4. Step back: name contradictions and the biggest pains/gains — those drive design.
- **Facilitator notes:** The power is forcing the team *out of its own head* into the stakeholder's senses — insist on concrete specifics ("sees a 9am alert backlog," not "is busy"). The richest insight is usually the gap between **Saying & Doing** and **Thinking & Feeling**; that contradiction is where unmet needs live. Complements `simple-ethnography` (this is a projected map; that is field observation) — use ethnography data to *fill* the map honestly rather than guessing.
