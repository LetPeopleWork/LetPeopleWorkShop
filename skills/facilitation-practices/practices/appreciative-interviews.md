# Appreciative Interviews (AI)

- **Purpose:** Discover the conditions that made past successes possible by having people tell each other real success stories, then mining those stories for the strengths and assets a group can build on.
- **When to use:** When you want to shift from deficit/problem-fixing to building on what already works; to surface hidden bright spots and the root causes of success worth spreading.
- **Group config:** Pairs (storytelling interview) -> groups of 4 (mining for assets) -> whole group harvest.
- **Timing:** ~60-90 min full run (paired interviews ~15 min each way, ~20 min mining in 4s, ~15 min harvest). Trim to ~40 min with one interview direction and tighter rounds.
- **Medium fit:**
  - In-person: pairs in comfortable corners telling stories aloud; quads on a wall capturing assets.
  - Miro: pair frames to note the story and its conditions, then a shared board to cluster strengths.
  - Video: long-ish breakout pairs for interviews, then groups of 4 to mine, then plenary share.
- **Steps:**
  1. Pose the prompt: "Tell about a time when [success / things worked at their best] — what happened?"
  2. In pairs, Person A interviews Person B about that story, drawing out concrete detail (then switch).
  3. In groups of 4, share the stories and dig for what made each success possible — conditions, strengths, assets.
  4. Whole group: harvest the root causes of success and the assets the group can amplify.
- **Facilitator notes:** The interviewer's job is to ask for *story and detail*, not opinions — "what exactly happened?" not "what do you think helps?" Do NOT let the mining step collapse into generic platitudes ("good communication"); push groups to name the specific, transferable conditions in each story. Protect the time — rushing the storytelling kills the warmth that makes it work.
