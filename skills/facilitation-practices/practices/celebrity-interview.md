# Celebrity Interview

- **Purpose:** Reconnect a leader or expert to the front line by *interviewing* them talk-show style instead of letting them present slides — a host draws out the human, the stories, and the candid answers the audience actually wants.
- **When to use:** All-hands and large gatherings where a leader/expert would otherwise lecture; when you want to humanize leadership, surface real questions, and avoid death-by-slides. Great for kicking off or anchoring a large event.
- **Group config:** One interviewee + one host on stage, large audience watching, then audience joins in (often via 1-2-4-All to generate and pose questions).
- **Timing:** ~45 min (host interview ~15-20 min, audience questions ~20 min, close). Trim to ~30 min with a tighter audience round.
- **Medium fit:**
  - In-person: excellent — stage, two chairs, a live audience. The talk-show staging is the trick.
  - Video: works well as a webinar-style interview; host and guest on camera, audience questions via chat or short breakouts feeding the host. Scales to large remote crowds.
  - Miro: not a presentation medium — this is a live conversation. Omit.
- **Steps:**
  1. Recruit a host and brief the interviewee that this is a conversation, not a talk — no slides.
  2. Gather the audience's real questions in advance (or live via 1-2-4-All) and shape them into the host's interview line.
  3. Host interviews the guest on stage — conversational, follow-up questions, draw out stories.
  4. Open to the audience: they ask their questions directly.
  5. Close by harvesting themes and any commitments.
- **Facilitator notes:** The host makes or breaks it — prep their questions *from the audience's* concerns, not from the leader's talking points, and coach them to follow up and gently push rather than lob softballs. The deal with the interviewee is "no slides, no prepared speech" — get that agreement explicitly beforehand or it reverts to a lecture. The format only earns its keep if the questions are real and a bit edgy; sanitized questions produce a sanitized non-event.
