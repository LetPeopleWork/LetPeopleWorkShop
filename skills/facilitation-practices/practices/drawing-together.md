# Drawing Together

- **Purpose:** Surface insights that words can't reach by having people *draw* their answer to a prompt using a simple shared visual language — then share the drawings to reveal patterns, tensions, and meaning underneath the talk.
- **When to use:** Stuck, abstract, or emotionally loaded topics where verbal discussion goes in circles; when you want to bypass the usual articulate-talkers and access intuition. Good for vision, journey, or "where are we really" questions.
- **Group config:** Silent individual drawing -> share in small groups (or pairs) -> whole-group harvest of patterns.
- **Timing:** ~40 min (5 min teach the five symbols, 10 min draw, 15 min share in small groups, 10 min harvest). Trim to ~25 min by sharing in pairs and shortening harvest.
- **Medium fit:**
  - In-person: excellent — paper and markers, drawings posted on the wall for a gallery walk.
  - Miro: strong fit — draw with the pen/shape tools or assemble the five symbols on the board; everyone draws in parallel on their own frame, then cluster. The board is built for silent visual work.
  - Video: weak without a shared canvas — drawing on paper then holding it to a webcam loses the share-and-compare. Prefer Miro for remote. Omit.
- **Steps:**
  1. Teach the five-element visual vocabulary (point/dot, line, angle, arc/curve, and a spiral or shape) — enough to draw anything. Reassure: this is symbols, not art.
  2. Pose the prompt. Each person draws their answer silently and individually.
  3. In small groups (or pairs), each person shows their drawing and the others say what they see *before* the artist explains.
  4. Harvest across the whole group: what images recur, what surprises, what the pictures say that words hadn't.
- **Facilitator notes:** The blocker is "I can't draw" anxiety — defuse it hard by teaching the five simple symbols and stressing that stick-figures and symbols are the point, not skill. Do the let-others-interpret-first step in sharing; the gap between what the artist meant and what others saw is where the insight is. Silence during the drawing is non-negotiable — protect it so people access intuition instead of narrating.
