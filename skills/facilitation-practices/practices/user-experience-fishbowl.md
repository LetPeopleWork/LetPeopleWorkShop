# User Experience Fishbowl

- **Purpose:** Transfer hard-won, tacit know-how from people who've actually done the thing to everyone else, by letting the experienced talk candidly among themselves while the rest listen in.
- **When to use:** A subset of the group holds practical experience others need (e.g. people who've survived a migration, a launch, a hard customer); you want the lived detail, not a polished presentation.
- **Group config:** Inner circle of ~4-6 experienced people; outer circle of everyone else listening. Optionally an open chair in the inner circle for a rotating listener.
- **Timing:** ~30 min for a single fishbowl (5 min setup, ~20 min inner discussion, harvest). Up to ~70 min with multiple topics, an open-chair rotation, and 1-2-4-All harvest. Trim by cutting the rotation.
- **Medium fit:**
  - In-person: two concentric rings of chairs, inner circle faces inward. Excellent — the physical geometry signals who's "on stage" and who listens.
  - Video: inner circle on camera/unmuted, everyone else cameras-off and muted, harvesting questions in chat. Works but weaker — you lose the felt presence of the listening ring and the easy open-chair swap; spotlight the speakers and gate chat so the inner talk isn't drowned.
  - Miro: poor fit — this is a live-discussion structure, not a board activity. Drop it.
- **Steps:**
  1. Invite the experienced people into the inner circle; everyone else forms the outer ring and is asked to listen, not interrupt.
  2. Pose 2-3 open questions to the inner circle (e.g. "What surprised you? What would you do differently?").
  3. The inner circle discusses among themselves, candidly, as if the outer ring weren't there. The outer ring stays silent and takes notes.
  4. (Optional) Keep one open chair inside; an outer-ring person may sit in to ask a question, then return.
  5. Harvest: outer ring shares what struck them, often via 1-2-4-All, and surfaces follow-up questions for the inner circle.
- **Facilitator notes:** The listening outer ring is essential — its silence is what frees the inner circle to be honest instead of presentational. Brief the outer ring explicitly that their job is to listen, not debate; the first time an outer-ring expert jumps in, the inner conversation freezes. Pick inner-circle members for genuine experience and candor, not rank.
