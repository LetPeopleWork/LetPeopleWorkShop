# Discovery & Action Dialogue (DAD)

- **Purpose:** Help a group discover and spread the solutions it *already has* by walking a small circle through seven set questions that surface positive deviants — the people quietly doing things that work.
- **When to use:** When a behavior or practice needs to change and top-down fixes haven't stuck; to find the home-grown workarounds that already exist inside the group and let them spread.
- **Group config:** One facilitated circle of ~7-15 (up to ~30 split into circles), discussing the same seven questions together.
- **Timing:** ~25-70 min depending on depth and group size — the seven questions pace it. Trim to ~25 min with a small circle and tight turns; expand toward 70 for larger or richer dialogue.
- **Medium fit:**
  - In-person: a single circle, facilitator posing the seven questions, capturing on a flip chart.
  - Miro: the seven questions as labeled frames, participants adding observations under each.
  - Video: one plenary circle (small enough that everyone speaks), questions shown on screen in turn.
- **Steps:**
  1. How do you know when [the problem] is present?
  2. How do you contribute effectively to solving [the problem]?
  3. What prevents you from doing this or taking these actions all the time?
  4. Do you know anybody who is able to frequently solve [the problem] and overcome barriers? What behaviors or practices made their success possible?
  5. Do you have any ideas?
  6. What needs to be done to make it happen? Any volunteers?
  7. Who else needs to be involved?
- **Facilitator notes:** Question 4 is the heart — it surfaces the positive deviants, the people already succeeding under the same constraints as everyone else. Do NOT let the group skate past it into generic brainstorming; press for *specific named behaviors* that real colleagues already do. Keep the circle small enough that quiet members actually speak, or the local solutions never come out.
