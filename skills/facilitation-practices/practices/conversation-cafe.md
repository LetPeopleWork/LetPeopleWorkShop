# Conversation Cafe

- **Purpose:** Help a group make calm, shared sense of a big or disturbing challenge through structured rounds where a talking object enforces one-voice-at-a-time, slowing everyone down to listen.
- **When to use:** After unsettling news or in the face of a complex, emotionally charged topic, when people need to engage rather than freeze or argue — to think together without rushing to solutions.
- **Group config:** Small groups of 5-7 seated in a circle, each with a talking object and (ideally) light cafe agreements.
- **Timing:** ~45-60 min full run (two talking-object rounds, open dialogue, closing round). Trim to ~30 min by shortening the open dialogue.
- **Medium fit:**
  - In-person: small circles with a real talking object (stone, marker); optional cafe etiquette card.
  - Miro: not ideal — the value is spoken, real-time presence; use only as a shared capture surface alongside voice.
  - Video: 5-7 person breakouts; the "talking object" is a strict hand-raise / pass order to keep one voice at a time.
- **Steps:**
  1. Introduce the agreements (be open, speak with your heart, listen, no need to agree).
  2. Round 1 (talking object): each person says what they think/feel about the topic — no cross-talk.
  3. Round 2 (talking object): each person responds to what they heard in round 1.
  4. Open dialogue: spirited conversation, still passing the talking object when it matters.
  5. Closing round (talking object): each person shares a takeaway.
- **Facilitator notes:** The talking object is the whole constraint — it stops interruption and forces real listening, which is exactly what a heated or scary topic needs. Do NOT drop it "because the group is mature"; the moment cross-talk returns, the loud and anxious voices take over and the calm sense-making evaporates. Insist the object is held in rounds 1, 2, and the close.
