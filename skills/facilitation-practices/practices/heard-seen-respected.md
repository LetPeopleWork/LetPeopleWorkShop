# Heard, Seen, Respected (HSR)

- **Purpose:** Build empathy and listening capacity by having people tell a story of a time they were *not* heard, seen, or respected — while their partner does nothing but listen, deeply, with no fixing or response.
- **When to use:** Before hard conversations, when a group needs to rebuild trust, or to practice the listening discipline that makes everything else (feedback, conflict, inclusion) work. Strong for diverse or fractured groups.
- **Group config:** Pairs, swapping teller/listener roles; then a whole-group harvest.
- **Timing:** ~25 min (2 min frame, two rounds of ~7 min each so both partners tell, ~5 min harvest). Trim to ~18 min with tighter rounds.
- **Medium fit:**
  - In-person: excellent — quiet pairs, full attention, no devices. Lower the ambient noise so listening can go deep.
  - Video: works in 2-person breakouts; ask listeners to turn off self-view and just attend. The constraint (no replying) actually translates well online.
  - Miro: not a board activity — this is spoken and listened. Omit.
- **Steps:**
  1. Frame it: this is about practicing listening, not solving. The listener will *not* respond, advise, or relate their own story.
  2. In pairs, one person tells a story of a time they felt unheard, unseen, or disrespected.
  3. The listener listens fully — silent, present, no fixing — for the full time.
  4. Swap roles and repeat.
  5. Harvest with the whole group: "What was it like to be listened to that way? What was hard about just listening?"
- **Facilitator notes:** The whole structure rises or falls on the *no-response* discipline — listeners will itch to nod-and-relate, advise, or say "me too," and that's exactly the habit being broken. Name it up front and reinforce it: their only job is to receive. Expect the "just listening was hard" admission in the harvest — that's the learning, not a failure. Keep the prompt about being unheard (not a happy story); the mild discomfort is what makes the empathy real.
