# Troika Consulting

- **Purpose:** Get practical help on a real challenge fast, through rounds of peer consulting in trios where each person takes a turn as the "client" and the other two as "consultants."
- **When to use:** When people have individual challenges they're stuck on and you want to tap the group's collective wisdom; builds a habit of asking for and giving help.
- **Group config:** Groups of 3 (one client, two consultants), rotating so each person gets a round. Needs multiples of 3 — handle remainders with one or two groups of 4.
- **Timing:** ~25-30 min for a full round of three (each client ~8-10 min: ~1-2 min ask, ~4-5 min consult, ~1-2 min reflect). Trim to ~20 min by tightening each turn.
- **Medium fit:**
  - In-person: trios in chairs; the client physically turns their chair away during consulting.
  - Miro: trio frames with the client's question posted; consultants type advice while client reads-only.
  - Video: 3-person breakouts; the client turns their camera off (or looks away) and mutes while consultants talk.
- **Steps:**
  1. Each person reflects on a challenge they want help with and frames it as a question.
  2. Round begins: the client states their challenge and question (~1-2 min), then *turns away / goes silent*.
  3. The two consultants ask each other clarifying questions, then generate ideas and advice while the client listens without responding (~4-5 min).
  4. The client turns back and shares what was most useful (~1-2 min).
  5. Rotate so each of the three takes a turn as client.
- **Facilitator notes:** The client *turning away and going silent* is the magic — it stops them defending, explaining, or steering, so the consultants actually riff freely and the client just listens. Do NOT let the client face the consultants and "discuss"; that collapses it into ordinary advice-giving and kills the gold. Enforce the silence and the turn.
