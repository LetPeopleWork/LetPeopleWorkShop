# Making-Space-with-TRIZ

- **Purpose:** Surface and let go of counterproductive things a group is *already doing*, by first imagining how to achieve the worst possible result — playful, depersonalizing, and safe for charged topics.
- **When to use:** Spicy / defensive topics where direct criticism would trigger people; when you suspect the group is unknowingly sabotaging its own goal.
- **Group config:** Whole group → small groups (1-2-4-All style) across three questions.
- **Timing:** ~20 min (3 rounds). Trim to ~12 min by tightening rounds.
- **Medium fit:**
  - In-person: stickies + wall, lots of laughter. Excellent for breaking tension.
  - Miro: three columns (worst-result list → "are we doing these?" → "what to stop").
  - Video: breakouts per question, shared doc.
- **Steps:**
  1. Ask: "What could we do to make cross-team work as dysfunctional as possible?" — list freely (this is fun; encourage extremes).
  2. Ask: "Go down the list — is there anything we're *already* doing that resembles these?" Mark honestly.
  3. Ask: "What's our first step to stop the most damaging one?"
- **Facilitator notes:** The reverse framing is what makes it safe — people criticize the *system*, not each other, and laugh while doing it. Powerful when the real problems are awkward to say out loud (e.g. territorial behavior). Do NOT skip step 2; the laugh in step 1 only converts to insight when they admit "…we kind of do that."
