# The Five-Phase Retrospective

- **Source detail:** Esther Derby & Diana Larsen, *Agile Retrospectives: Making Good Teams Great* (Pragmatic Bookshelf, 2006), Ch. 1. The canonical structure for a repeatable team-learning ritual ("inspect and adapt"). A 2024 2nd edition exists; a hypothesis-driven six-phase variant circulates — treat the original five as canon.
- **Purpose:** Give any retrospective a reliable **shape** — from a shared picture of what happened, to *why*, to one or two concrete experiments — bracketed by safety setup and a retro-on-the-retro, so a team reflects and improves without descending into blame or a solution free-for-all.
- **When to use:** As the macro-arc for any **reflection / improvement** session on a team's own way of working (iteration retro, project review, post-incident learning). Map grounded structures into each phase; vary the activities, keep the arc.
- **Medium fit:** all — it's a sequencing backbone, not an activity. (Note: it differs from the decision arc and the learning arc — see below.)

## The five phases
1. **Set the Stage** — get *everyone* to speak early and create enough safety to be honest (silence is otherwise tacitly permitted). → map: `esvp`, working agreements (`min-specs`), a check-in (`heard-seen-respected`).
2. **Gather Data** — build a shared picture of what actually happened — **hard facts and feelings** ("feelings are at least half the story"). → map: `mad-sad-glad`, `team-radar`, a timeline.
3. **Generate Insights** — ask *why*; analyze causes and effects **before** jumping to solutions (the most-skipped, most-important phase). → map: `learning-matrix`, root-cause analysis, the "So What?" of `what-so-what-now-what`.
4. **Decide What to Do** — pick **no more than one or two** experiments and make them concrete and owned. → map: `15-percent-solutions`, `gradients-of-agreement`, `min-specs`.
5. **Close the Retrospective** — end *decisively*; decide how to retain learnings; appreciate; **retrospect the retrospective**. → map: `plus-delta`, `roti`, appreciations (`heard-seen-respected`).

**Time guide (1-hour retro):** Stage ~5 · Data ~10 · Insights ~20 · Decide ~15 · Close ~5. Scales from one hour to multiple days by lengthening Data/Insights.

## How to use it
Keep the arc constant and swap activities per phase for variety. Don't collapse Gather Data into Generate Insights — a shared picture must exist before you analyze it, or people just defend their own version. And don't let the group solve in phase 2 or 3.

## Facilitator notes
The discipline the arc enforces: **data before judgement, judgement before solutions, and few experiments not many** — "too many initiatives overwhelm your ability to change." The most common failure is skipping Generate Insights and leaping from data straight to a to-do list — protect that phase. This is distinct from `participatory-decision-diamond` (a *decision* arc: options → Groan Zone → choose) and `tbr-4cs` (a *teaching* arc: deliver and practice new content); the retro's raw material is the team's own recent experience, and its close is uniquely a retro-on-the-retro. Cite Derby & Larsen.
