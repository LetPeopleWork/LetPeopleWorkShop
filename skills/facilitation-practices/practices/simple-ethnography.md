# Simple Ethnography

- **Purpose:** Reveal what people actually do — not what they say they do — by sending participants into the field to observe real users or real work, then bringing the raw behavior back to make sense of it together.
- **When to use:** You suspect a gap between stated process and lived practice, or you're designing for users whose real behavior the group only assumes; great for grounding strategy in evidence rather than opinion.
- **Group config:** Pairs or small groups go observe in the field, then reconvene to debrief and harvest patterns to All.
- **Timing:** ~80-120 min including fieldwork (brief ~10 min, observe 30-60 min in the field, debrief + harvest ~40 min). Trim toward ~80 min by using pre-gathered observations or shadowing nearby; the field portion is what costs the time.
- **Medium fit:**
  - In-person / field: pairs go to where the work or use actually happens, observe quietly, take field notes, return to debrief. Excellent — and essentially the only faithful mode; the structure requires leaving the room.
  - Miro: only as the debrief surface (clustering field notes) once people are back — not for the observation itself.
  - Video: poor fit for the core mechanic — remote "observation" loses the embodied detail. Skip unless observing an inherently on-screen workflow.
- **Steps:**
  1. Brief observers: your job is to watch *behavior* — what people do, in what order, what they touch, where they hesitate — not to interview or judge. Capture concrete field notes.
  2. Send pairs into the field to observe a real user, customer, or work process for a set window. Watch and note; ask only minimal clarifying questions if any.
  3. Reconvene. Each pair shares their raw observations — specific actions seen, surprises, frictions — before any interpretation.
  4. Cluster the observations and look for patterns: recurring workarounds, gaps between intended and actual, unmet needs.
  5. Harvest implications and next steps to the whole group.
- **Facilitator notes:** The discipline is observing behavior, not collecting opinions — the instant pairs start interviewing or theorizing in the field, you get the same self-reported story you were trying to escape. Insist that debrief begins with concrete "I saw X do Y" before anyone interprets; raw observation first, meaning second. It genuinely requires leaving the room, so protect the fieldwork time and don't let it collapse into a thought experiment.
