# 15% Solutions

- **Purpose:** Turn a big, stuck problem into action by having each person name what they can do *right now*, with no more resources, budget, or permission than they already have — their 15%.
- **When to use:** When people feel powerless or stuck waiting on others; to convert big aspirations into immediate, ownable first steps and build personal accountability.
- **Group config:** Self (solo list) -> small groups of 2-4 (share + consult).
- **Timing:** ~20 min (~5 min solo, ~10-12 min sharing/advising in small groups, ~3 min harvest). Trim to ~12 min with shorter sharing.
- **Medium fit:**
  - In-person: silent solo listing on paper, then read-arounds in trios with peer advice.
  - Miro: private frame per person for their 15% list, then small-group frames to give feedback.
  - Video: solo write in a doc, then breakout trios to share and consult, then plenary.
- **Steps:**
  1. Each person works alone: "Where do you have discretion and freedom to act? What can you do without more resources or authority?" List your 15% Solutions.
  2. In small groups, each person describes one 15% Solution; the group asks clarifying questions.
  3. The group offers advice and consultation on how to strengthen each person's 15%.
  4. Harvest the most promising first steps.
- **Facilitator notes:** The constraint "no new resources, no new authority" is the whole point — the moment someone says "well, IF we had budget..." they've left their 15% and re-entered the land of waiting. Hold the line firmly. The list of *small* things they can start Monday is what produces movement, not the grand plan.
