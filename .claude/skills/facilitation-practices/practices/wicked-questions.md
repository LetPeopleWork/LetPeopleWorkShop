# Wicked Questions

- **Source:** Liberating Structures — https://www.liberatingstructures.com/4-wicked-questions/
- **Purpose:** Name a paradox the group must hold (two things that are both true and in tension) without resolving it prematurely — makes a hidden either/or discussable.
- **When to use:** When a group is polarizing toward one "solution" and you want to surface the real tension instead. Strong alternative to TRIZ for this kind of session.
- **Group config:** 1-2-4-All to draft and refine the paradox question.
- **Timing:** ~20 min.
- **Medium fit:** In-person (wall), Miro (frame), Video (shared doc) — all fine.
- **Steps:**
  1. Offer the template: "How is it that we are X and we are also Y at the same time?"
  2. Groups draft wicked questions about cross-team work (e.g. autonomy vs. alignment).
  3. Harvest the sharpest ones; sit with them — do not solve.
- **Facilitator notes:** Use this **instead of** TRIZ when the room is more analytical than defensive, or the convener's "one big team" framing is already pulling people into a premature either/or. It legitimizes the tension without taking sides.
