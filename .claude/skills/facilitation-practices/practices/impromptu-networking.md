# Impromptu Networking

- **Source:** Liberating Structures — https://www.liberatingstructures.com/4-impromptu-networking/
- **Purpose:** Rapidly engage everyone in voicing why they're here and surface lived experience; build connection and equal footing before harder work.
- **When to use:** Opening a session; warming a group up; equalizing voice so quiet people are already "in" before the main work.
- **Group config:** Pairs, standing, rotating partners each round.
- **Timing:** ~15 min (3 rounds × ~4 min + setup). Trim to 10 min with 2 rounds.
- **Medium fit:**
  - In-person: standing, mingle, ring a bell to rotate. Best energy.
  - Miro: not ideal (pairing friction) — prefer video breakouts.
  - Video: 1:1 breakout rooms, reshuffle each round.
- **Steps:**
  1. Pose one question people answer in pairs (e.g. an invitation to share a concrete recent experience).
  2. ~4 min per pair, then rotate to a new partner. Repeat for 2-3 rounds.
- **Facilitator notes:** Keep the question concrete and experience-based, not opinion-based — it lowers the stakes. Do not debrief in plenary; the value is the connection + priming.
